Eastern Fox Squirrel

Specifically needed is a photograph of Sciurus niger vulpinus.

Photographs I have found online claiming to be this subspecies that could potentially used either have a stated locale outside the range of this subspecies, or do not state a locale and look suspiciously like Sciurus niger rufiventer.

This squirrel use to be present in the southen part of Pennsylvania but apparently is either exceedingly rare there now, or has been extirpated.

It is reportedly still present within six counties of North Carolina at the northern end of the Blue Mountain range, and also in Virgina west of the Blue Mountains.

My preference would be from North Carolina part of the range in a state park away from heavy urban areas, as Sciurus niger rufiventer is often introduced in urban areas. There are several state parks there, I am guessing one of them has a healthy population.

See Fox Squirrel—North Carolina Wildlife Profiles (PDF) for a range map.

Note that two subspecies of Sciurus niger are in North Carolina. S. n. vulpinus is the smaller range shown on the map, where North Carolina hits Tennessee and Virginia.

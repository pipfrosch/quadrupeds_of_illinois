Glossary of Terms

abscess
A cavity caused by tissue destruction, usually because of infection, filled with pus and surrounded by inflamed tissue. Wiktionary 2020 August 03
allele
A genetic variation of a gene. Please not that in non-academic conversation, the word gene is often used where the word allele should be used. For example, we all have the same genes, what makes people have genetically individual characterists are different variations of those genes we all have, and those variations are properly referred to as alleles, not genes.
arboreal
Dwelling primarily in the trees.
arvicolae
The author uses this term interchangeably with meadow-mice, which are now often referred to as voles.
binomial name
Sometimes also referred to as ‘binomial nomenclature’. The specified taxonomy Genus and Species assigned to a biological life form. For example, the Gray Wolf has the binomial name Canis lupus. See also trinomial name.
bring forth
An idiom to indicate the bearing of young.
carnivorous
Feeds upon animals.
conchologist
One who studies mollusk shells.
congener
A member of the same taxonomy genus.
congenial
pleasant and familiar, safe.
corn crib
A building originally designed by the indigenous peoples of the Americas and later adopted by the European colonies who then spread the design back to Europe. It is designed to protect harvested corn—still on the cob—from the elements while retaining outside air flow, allowing the corn to dry. Corn cribs are usually elevated to reduce predation by pests.
corn shock
Before modern mechanized harvesting techniques were developed, in the fall when corn was harvested it often be dried by bundling corn stalks together vertically and left in the field to dry. These bundles were called corn shocks. See Photo 24. The Amish still use this technique.
cryptic species complex
A group of two or more closely related yet distinct species that are either difficult or impossible to distinguish from each other based upon morphological features alone.
depredation
Consumption of agricultural resources to the loss of the farmer.
The author frequently uses this term incorrectly to simply mean tamed; living as a pet that has learned to accept captivity. The actual meaning however is a lineage of plants or animals that have been through a process of selective breeding by humans so that they now differ, often considerably, from their wild form for a beneficial purpose to man; often with the result of changed natural history should they be required to survive without man caring for them.
domiciled
Established as resident.
enemy
In the context of natural history, a species that kills another species, often but not always for food, is sometimes referred to as an ‘enemy’.
et cetera
An indication that a list continues with additional items that are similar to the previously specified items. Typically abbreviated, as &c. historically but more commonly in the present with etc.
fecundity
Number, rate, or capacity of offspring production. Wiktionary 2020 July 08
forenoon
Archaic synonym for morning; the time between sunrise and noon.
girdle
To kill or stunt a tree by removing or inverting a ring of bark. Wiktionary 2020 July 08
gravid
Synonymous with pregnant. In recent years, it is no longer customary to use gravid in the context of mammals, but it is still frequently used in the context of reptiles, live-bearing fish, and some invertebrates.
haunts
locations of habitation.
horde
A numerically large grouping of organisms; typically used in the context of a migration from one location to another.
idiom
An established expression whose meaning is not deducible from the literal meanings of its component words, often peculiar to a given language. Wiktionary 2020 August 03
inclement
Severe; unrelenting. Usually used in the context of weather.
indigenous peoples
The ethnic people groups who inhabited a land prior to the recording of modern written history. Also often referred to as ‘First Nations’ or ‘Native Peoples’.
injurious
harmful; causing loss of crops or livestock in the context of farming.
ITIS
Abbreviation for Intergrated Taxonomic Information System. An online database with “authoritative taxonomic information on plants, animals, fungi, and microbes of North America and the world.” The database is available to the public at https://www.itis.gov/.
larva
Usually used with insects and amphibians. A life stage after birth or hatching but prior to metamorphosis into the adult form. Pluralized as larvae.
mamma
The milk secreting organ of mammals. Pluralized as mammae.
mast
In the context of botany, the fruit of forest trees and shrubs; acorns and other nuts.
masticate
To chew, often in the context of extended chewing, such as the chewing of cud.
melanin
Any of a group of naturally occurring dark pigments, especially the pigment found in skin, hair, fur, and feathers.Wiktionary 2020 August 03
melanism
The excessive production of melanin in an organism rulting in a black or very dark coloration that is often atypical for the species.
melanistic
Having the property of melanism
metamorphosis
The morphological transition some animals go through from one life stage into their next (often adult) life stage.
milldam
A dam constructed to create a mill pond, using water that overflows the dam to turn a water wheel.
milk (corn)
A stage during the development of corn where the kernels are mostly yellow and contain a milky white fluid. See the Purdue University web page Kernel Development Stages in Corn for more information.
morphological
The observable physical characteristics of an organism.
myriad
Large quantity or number.
Name Authority
Often just referred to as ‘authority’. When a taxonomy rank, binomial name, or trinomial name is specified; the name of the naturalist considered to be the authority for that rank or name is often also specified. Usually the name of the naturalist and the year of the authoritative description is given, in some cases the ITIS Taxonomic Serial Number (TSN) is also specified, when one exists. When an authority is used with a binomial name, the authority usually refers to the naturalist associated with describing the Species rank even if the Genus rank differs from their description. When used with a trinomial name, the authority usually referes to the naturalist associated with describing the Subspecies rank, even if that naturalist originally assigned it as a now-invalidated Species rank. It should be noted that in some cases, especially where taxonomy confusion exists, the authority may not reference the first naturalist to use the rank name. Pipfrosch Press always encapsulates the authority within square brackets. In zoology, sometimes the abbreviation L. is used in an authority to indicate Linnaeus (or Linné if after 1761 when he was granted nobility status). Botany has some additional common authority abbreviations.
pecuniary
Fiscally or monetary.
plumbeous
Of a dull gray lead-like color.
pomologist
A person who studies fruits and the cultivation of of fruits.
pugnacious
Naturally aggressive or hostile. Wiktionary 2020 July 09
pupa
The life stage of an insect during the metamorphosis transition, often taking place within a shell the larva creates called a cocoon. Pluralized as pupae.
quadruped
In the general sense, any animal that uses four limbs for locomotion. It differs from the term tetrapod in that tetrapods include animals that at one point in their evolutionary history had four limbs but no longer do—such as snakes—and tetrapod also includes species with four limbs but have adapted to bipedal (two limb) locomotion. However even though the quadruped technically applies to many reptiles and amphibians, quadruped is generally used to refer to mammals that use four limbs for locomotion.
rapacious
Predatory, typically in reference to predators that hunt down and seize live prey, usually in reference to vertebrate prey. In modern times, this term is usually only used in the context of rapacious birds but historically it also has been heavily used in the context of rapacious mammals.
reconnoiter
To perform a reconnaissance (of an area; an enemy position); to scout with the aim of gaining information. Wiktionary 2020 July 27
rod
When used as a unit of measure, a rod is 16½ feet which is approximately five meters.
rudimentary
Basic or minimal. Archaic form: rudimental.
scientific name
The taxonomy binomial or trinomial name assigned to a species or subspecies.
spermophile
Ground squirrels and burrowing squirrel-like rodents. See page 73b.
taxonomy
A system of grouping life forms into hierarchical categories to assist in their study. The standard categories, in hierarchical order, are Kingdom, Phylum, Class, Order, Family, Genus, and Species. In some cases, super or sub prefixes are used with those categories to indicate an additional category level before or after one of those categories, with ‘Subspecies’ being the most commonly encountered additional category by the layman.
trinomial name
Sometimes also referred to as ‘trinomial nomenclature’. The specified taxonomy Genus, Species, and Subpecies assigned to a biological life form. For example, the Mexican Gray Wolf has the trinomial name Canis lupus baileyi. See also binomial name.
